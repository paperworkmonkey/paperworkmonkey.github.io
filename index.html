Initial guidance:

  1. Enter the patient's actual height and gender to calculate their ideal body weight (IBW).
  2. You can then select from actual body weight (ABW), adjusted body weight (Adj BW), BMI 27 weight, or ideal body weight (IBW) by clicking the corresponding button.
  3. Adjust the energy requirements using the slider provided. The default value is set to 25 kcal/kg/day for energy. In the early catabolic phase of critical illness a value of 20-25 kcal/kg/day is appropriate. Later, in the anabolic phase between 30 and 35 kcal/kg/day can be used.
  4. Adjust the protein requirements using the slider provided. The default value is set to 1.5 g/kg/day. In critically ill patients, protein requirements may range from 1.2 to 2.0 g/kg/day depending on the clinical situation.
  5. If the patient is receiving naloxegol (or any other medication requiring a 3 hour break in feed administration), tick the naloxegol checkbox to adjust feed rates to account for a 3 hour break in feed administration.
  6. If the patient is receiving propofol, enter an approximate hourly rate to account for the additional calories provided by the lipid emulsion which is 1.1 kcal/ml irrespective of whether 1% or 2% propofol is used. The calculator will convert the hourly rate to a 24 hour equivalent calorie value.
  7. If the patient is hypernatraemic or on a sodium-restricted diet, tick the Na restrict checkbox to highlight feed regimens that have higher sodium content (>0.5mmol/kg/day).
  • Values highlighted in red suggest that these feeds should be reviewed carefully as they may not be clinically appropriate.
  • Select a feed by clicking anywhere in the relevant column. Copy and paste the feed details to your patient's chart.
